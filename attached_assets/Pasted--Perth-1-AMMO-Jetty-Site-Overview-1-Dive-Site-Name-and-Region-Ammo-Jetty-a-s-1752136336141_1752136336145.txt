**2. Image Header**

## üåä Conditions

**8. Best Time to Dive**

**10. Visibility**

**15. Seasonal Events**

## 2. Blackwall Reach üåç **Site Overview**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Blackwall Reach**, located along the Swan River in the Perth metropolitan area, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Images show a calm river scene with visible mooring buoys (527 and 716) and dense trees lining the steep banks. Some photos highlight entry points and underwater wreckage.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for Open Water certified divers. While it‚Äôs relatively shallow, variable visibility and river currents make it better suited for divers with moderate experience and good navigation skills.
    
4. **Location Description and Coordinates: 32¬∞01'16.4"S 115¬∞46'58.9"E**
    
    The site is situated at the end of Blackwall Reach Parade near Point Walter, about a 20-minute drive from central Perth. It‚Äôs outside a designated marine park but lies within a popular recreational reserve along the Swan River.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    This is a **shore-based** dive. Divers walk through a bush track and wade approximately 200 meters before descending into the water.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    The site ranges from shallow shoreline entries to around **15 metres** at its deepest in the central river channel.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Entry is via a small beach. Conditions can be silty underfoot, and divers may need to surface swim out to buoy markers before descending.
    

---

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Diving is best in stable weather, avoiding days after rainfall which can release tannins and silt into the water. Calm, cool-weather days provide the clearest conditions.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    River temperatures typically range from **16¬∞C to 22¬∞C**, following seasonal patterns similar to Perth‚Äôs coastal waters.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Visibility is usually between **1 to 4 metres**, often reduced after storms or rain due to runoff and stirred sediment. On exceptional days it can reach 5 metres.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Currents are generally mild near the bank but increase further into the river. Be mindful of boat traffic and ferry wake, which can cause surface disturbance.
    
5. **Real-Time Data Suggestion**
    
    Linking to Swan River tide and turbidity data (via BOM or local council APIs) would help divers plan safely.
    

---

## üê† **Marine Life & Ecosystem (Featured Species)**

1. **Marine Species Commonly Seen**
    
    Diverse riverine species include bream, stripeys, moon and white-spotted jellyfish, gobbleguts, tube worms, and occasional crabs or shrimp.
    
2. **Habitat Type**
    
    This is a **freshwater‚Äìestuarine ecosystem** with submerged artificial structures like barges and cars that form a kind of urban wreck reef.
    
3. **Seasonal Marine Life Events**
    
    Jellyfish blooms are more common in warmer months, and fish activity can vary with water clarity and oxygen levels.
    
4. **Conservation Info**
    
    While not a marine park, the area falls within a recreational reserve. Divers must use a surface marker buoy and be cautious of nearby watercraft.
    

---

## üó∫Ô∏è **Dive Map**

1. **Dive Site Layout**
    
    Typical dives begin at buoy 527 and proceed along a shallow wall, heading toward buoy 716. Divers often encounter several wrecks ‚Äî sunken cars, old barges, and river debris ‚Äî spread across a silty bottom.
    
2. **Community Notes**
    
    Local divers recommend using a torch and gloves. The wrecks attract macro life, but visibility can change quickly. Use grid-style navigation and track landmarks to avoid getting disoriented.
    

---

## üì∏ **Extras**

1. **Visual Media**
    
    Photos show jellyfish, rusted wreckage, and typical murky water conditions. Videos highlight macro life like gobbleguts and anemones attached to submerged debris.
    
2. **Unique or Memorable Features**
    
    This site is one of Perth‚Äôs only accessible **freshwater wreck dives**, blending a raw urban setting with surprising biological diversity.
    
3. **User Experience**
    
    Divers mention that the site feels like a treasure hunt ‚Äî murky but fascinating, with unexpected species and eerie underwater structures. However, good buoyancy and navigation skills are a must.
    

---

## üìò **Learn Section**

1. **Mini-Lesson 1: ‚ÄúLife Among Wrecks ‚Äì How Submerged Structures Become Urban Reefs‚Äù**
    
    Explore how sunken objects like cars and barges transform into microhabitats, supporting diverse marine species even in non-traditional environments.
    
2. **Mini-Lesson 2: ‚ÄúRiver Health and Water Clarity ‚Äì What Affects Visibility in Estuarine Dives?‚Äù**
    
    Understand how rain, runoff, boat activity, and water chemistry affect visibility and life in river dive sites like Blackwall Reach.

## 3. üåç **Site Overview: Bulk Jetty**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Bulk Jetty**, located in **Kwinana**, just south of Perth, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Photos typically show the long industrial jetty extending into Cockburn Sound, with pylons disappearing into the blue-green water‚Äîoften revealing schooling fish and marine growth along the structure.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for **beginners to intermediate** divers. Entry and navigation are relatively easy, but awareness of current and visibility is important.
    
4. **Location Description and coordinates: 32¬∞12'36.0"S 115¬∞46'02.3"E**
    
    Near **Kwinana Beach**, in the industrial maritime zone south of Fremantle. Not within a marine park. No GPS coordinates listed, but access is straightforward from Kwinana Beach car parks.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    **Shore dive**, with a surface swim to the jetty structure. Be prepared for a walk across the beach and shallow entry.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    Varies from **3 to 10 metres**, depending on tide and distance along the jetty.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Enter from the beach‚Äîsoft sand with a gradual slope. Watch for boat traffic near the jetty, and swim to the pylons before descending.
    

---

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Ideal during **low wind conditions and slack tide**. Early mornings often provide the calmest water and best visibility.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    Typically ranges from **18¬∞C (winter)** to **24¬∞C (summer)**.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Generally **5‚Äì10 metres**, but can drop due to swell or sediment. After storms, visibility is significantly reduced.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Light current along the jetty, though surge can build near the entrance if the wind picks up. Be mindful of boat wash and swell in exposed areas.
    

---

## üê† **Marine Life & Ecosystem**

1. **Marine Species Commonly Seen**
    - Schools of **scalyfin**, **bullseyes**, and **herring**
    - **Octopuses**, **seahorses**, and **cuttlefish** hiding in pylons
    - Occasional sightings of **port jackson sharks** or **stingrays**
    - **Nudibranchs** and **soft corals** on the pylon structure
2. **Habitat Type**
    
    **Artificial reef**, formed by jetty pylons and industrial structure colonised by marine growth and invertebrates.
    
3. **Seasonal Marine Life Events**
    
    Warmer months may bring more **juvenile fish**, and spring‚Äìsummer may see **cuttlefish and squid** activity.
    
4. **Conservation Info**
    
    Not a declared marine park, but general rules around fishing and marine life handling apply. Divers should be cautious and avoid touching or disturbing marine organisms.
    

---

## üó∫Ô∏è **Dive Map**

1. **Dive Site Layout**
    
    The jetty runs straight into the ocean with evenly spaced pylons. Divers usually follow the **left or right side of the structure**, exploring marine growth on pylons, checking under crossbeams, and looking for cryptic species along the seabed. Swim out on the surface, descend at a central point, and return along the opposite side.
    
2. **Community Notes**
    
    Local divers suggest **avoiding busy times due to boat traffic**. Some have shared **sketches of the pylon layout**, which can be helpful for planning entry/exit points.
    

---

## üì∏ **Extras**

1. **Visual Media**
    
    Photos on the site highlight schooling fish between pylons, macro life like nudibranchs and seahorses, and the industrial contrast of rusting beams and soft corals.
    
2. **Unique or Memorable Features**
    
    The contrast between industrial structure and natural marine colonisation makes Bulk Jetty a great urban ecology dive. Good for photography, especially macro.
    
3. **User Experience**
    
    Divers often describe it as **surprisingly rich in life**, despite the industrial location. It's appreciated for **easy access, reliable sightings**, and low depth range. However, **visibility can be hit or miss** depending on recent weather.
    

---

## üìò **Learn Section**

Here are two mini-lesson topics inspired by the dive site:

1. **Artificial Reefs & Urban Marine Habitats**
    
    Learn how man-made structures like jetties, shipwrecks, and sea walls become important marine ecosystems, attracting fish, algae, and invertebrates.
    
2. **Cephalopods of Cockburn Sound**
    
    Discover more about the cuttlefish, octopuses, and squid species found in southern WA‚Äîhow they use camouflage, their intelligence, and why they love places like Bulk Jetty.
    

## 4. Camilla Wreck üåç **Site Overview**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Camilla Wreck**, located in **Cockburn Sound**, near **Kwinana**, south of Perth, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Photographs showcase remnants of the wreckage nestled in shallow waters, surrounded by paddle grass and sandy seabed, offering glimpses of marine life like sea anemones.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for **beginners and snorkelers**. The shallow depth and straightforward access make it ideal for those new to diving.
    
4. **Location Description and coordinates: 32¬∞11'15.6"S 115¬∞46'27.2"E**
    
    Accessed via **Challenger Beach**, off **Sutton Road**, which branches from **Cockburn Road**. The wreck lies approximately 80 meters offshore, near the **ALCOA Jetty**.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    A **shore dive**, involving a beach entry followed by a short surface swim to the wreck site.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    Maximum depth at the wreck is around **3.6 meters**, with surrounding areas being shallower.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Enter from the sandy beach, walking north approximately 120 meters to a noticeable erosion point in the dunes, then heading west into the water.
    

---

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Optimal during **calm weather** with minimal wind and swell. The site is exposed and lacks protection from rough conditions.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    Typically ranges from **18¬∞C in winter** to **24¬∞C in summer**, consistent with Cockburn Sound's temperatures.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Generally **low to moderate**, affected by silty conditions and easily disturbed sediment. Careful finning is essential to maintain clarity.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Minimal currents due to the shallow depth, but the area is near a working jetty, so be cautious of boat traffic and potential swells.
    

---

## üê† **Marine Life & Ecosystem**

1. **Marine Species Commonly Seen**
    - Small schools of **stripeys**, **cardinalfish**, and **wavy grubfish**
    - **Sea anemones** in paddle grass areas
    - Occasional sightings of **octopuses** and **nudibranchs**
2. **Habitat Type**
    
    A **shallow wreck** surrounded by **paddle grass** and **sandy seabed**, providing a habitat for various small marine species.
    
3. **Seasonal Marine Life Events**
    
    No specific seasonal events noted, but warmer months may see increased activity among resident species.
    
4. **Conservation Info**
    
    The site is protected under **Maritime Legislation**. Divers should not disturb or remove any artifacts.
    

---

## üó∫Ô∏è **Dive Map**

1. **Dive Site Layout**
    
    From the beach entry point, swim approximately 80 meters west to reach the wreck. The wreck itself is about 10 meters in length, lying in shallow water, with surrounding paddle grass beds.
    
2. **Community Notes**
    
    Divers recommend using a **dive flag** due to proximity to boat traffic. The site is also suitable for **snorkeling**, given its shallow depth.
    

---

## üì∏ **Extras**

1. **Visual Media**
    
    Photos and videos depict the wreck's remains, sea anemones in paddle grass, and the general layout of the site.
    
2. **Unique or Memorable Features**
    
    The **ease of access** and **shallow depth** make it a unique site for beginners and those interested in maritime history.
    
3. **User Experience**
    
    Divers describe it as a **simple and accessible dive**, though visibility can be limited. The site's historical context adds interest despite its modest marine life.
    

---

## üìò **Learn Section**

1. **Maritime History of the Camilla**
    
    Explore the journey of the Camilla, a 190-ton twin-masted schooner launched in 1834, which ended its service as a hulk before being scuttled in 1903.
    
2. **Paddle Grass Ecosystems**
    
    Understand the role of paddle grass in coastal marine environments, including its importance as a habitat for various marine species.
