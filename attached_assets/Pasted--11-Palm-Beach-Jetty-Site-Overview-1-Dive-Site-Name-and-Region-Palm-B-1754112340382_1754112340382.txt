## 11. Palm Beach Jetty üåç **Site Overview**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Palm Beach Jetty**, located in **Rockingham**, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Photographs depict the 125-meter-long jetty extending into the waters of Mangles Bay, with the surrounding area showcasing calm, shallow waters and sandy beaches.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for **novice divers and snorkelers**. The shallow depths and easy access make it ideal for beginners, though the site's limited marine life may not appeal to more experienced divers.
    
4. **Location Description/Coordinates: -32.276486, 115.719971**
    
    Situated at the end of **Fisher Street** in Rockingham, approximately 45 minutes south of Perth CBD. The site is near the **Rockingham Wreck Trail**, offering additional diving opportunities in the vicinity.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    **Shore dive**, with entry from the beach adjacent to the jetty.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    Depths range from **2 to 15 meters**, with the diveable length of the jetty being approximately 60 meters.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Enter from the sandy beach area, taking care to avoid sharp rocks. Be cautious of waves breaking over the reef, and avoid diving during high swell conditions.

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Optimal conditions occur during **calm weather** with minimal swell and easterly winds. Early mornings are often best, as winds tend to pick up later in the day.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    Typically ranges from **18¬∞C in winter** to **24¬∞C in summer**, consistent with regional averages.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Visibility can vary; while it can exceed **20 meters** on calm days, it's commonly around **6 meters**, influenced by swell and wind conditions.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Generally minimal currents, but surge can be significant, especially near the reef top. Divers should be cautious to avoid being lifted onto shallow reef areas.
