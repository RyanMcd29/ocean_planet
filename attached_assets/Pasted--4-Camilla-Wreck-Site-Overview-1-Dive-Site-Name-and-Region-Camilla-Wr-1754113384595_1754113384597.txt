## 4. Camilla Wreck üåç **Site Overview**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Camilla Wreck**, located in **Cockburn Sound**, near **Kwinana**, south of Perth, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Photographs showcase remnants of the wreckage nestled in shallow waters, surrounded by paddle grass and sandy seabed, offering glimpses of marine life like sea anemones.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for **beginners and snorkelers**. The shallow depth and straightforward access make it ideal for those new to diving.
    
4. **Location Description and coordinates: 32¬∞11'15.6"S 115¬∞46'27.2"E**
    
    Accessed via **Challenger Beach**, off **Sutton Road**, which branches from **Cockburn Road**. The wreck lies approximately 80 meters offshore, near the **ALCOA Jetty**.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    A **shore dive**, involving a beach entry followed by a short surface swim to the wreck site.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    Maximum depth at the wreck is around **3.6 meters**, with surrounding areas being shallower.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Enter from the sandy beach, walking north approximately 120 meters to a noticeable erosion point in the dunes, then heading west into the water.
    

---

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Optimal during **calm weather** with minimal wind and swell. The site is exposed and lacks protection from rough conditions.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    Typically ranges from **18¬∞C in winter** to **24¬∞C in summer**, consistent with Cockburn Sound's temperatures.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Generally **low to moderate**, affected by silty conditions and easily disturbed sediment. Careful finning is essential to maintain clarity.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Minimal currents due to the shallow depth, but the area is near a working jetty, so be cautious of boat traffic and potential swells.

## üê† **Marine Life & Ecosystem**

1. **Marine Species Commonly Seen**
    - Small schools of **stripeys**, **cardinalfish**, and **wavy grubfish**
    - **Sea anemones** in paddle grass areas
    - Occasional sightings of **octopuses** and **nudibranchs**
2. **Habitat Type**
    
    A **shallow wreck** surrounded by **paddle grass** and **sandy seabed**, providing a habitat for various small marine species.
    
3. **Seasonal Marine Life Events**
    
    No specific seasonal events noted, but warmer months may see increased activity among resident species.
    
4. **Conservation Info**
    
    The site is protected under **Maritime Legislation**. Divers should not disturb or remove any artifacts.

1. **Dive Site Layout**

2. **Community Notes**
    
    Divers recommend using a **dive flag** due to proximity to boat traffic. The site is also suitable for **snorkeling**, given its shallow depth.

## **Jervoise Bay Groin** üåç **Site Overview**

1. **Dive Site Name and Region**
    
    **Jervoise Bay Groin**, located near **Woodman Point**, south of Perth, Western Australia.
    
2. **Image Header**
    
    Images depict the man-made rock groins and concrete jetty structures extending into the bay, providing shelter and habitat for marine life.
    
3. **Diver Experience Level & Certification Needed**
    
    Suitable for **novice to intermediate** divers. The site offers sheltered conditions, especially during poor weather, making it accessible for less experienced divers.
    
4. **Location Description and coordinates: 32¬∞08'22.5"S 115¬∞45'40.4"E**
    
    Situated adjacent to the **Cockburn Power Boat Club** in **Jervoise Bay**, near **Woodman Point**. Access is via **O'Kane Court**, off **Cockburn Road**. The site is approximately 50 minutes from Perth CBD, 20 minutes from Fremantle, and 20 minutes from Rockingham.
    
5. **Access Type**
    
    **Shore dive**. Divers can enter from the beach or by carefully navigating over rocks along the groin structures.
    
6. **Depth Range**
    
    Depths reach up to **10 meters** at the shore end, potentially increasing further along the groin wall.
    
7. **Entry Conditions**
    
    Entry points vary; some involve walking over uneven, jagged rocks, while others offer beach access. Divers should assess entry and exit points based on personal ability and be cautious of boat traffic and fishing activities.
    

---

## üåä **Conditions**

1. **Best Time to Dive**
    
    Ideal during **poor weather conditions**, as the site is sheltered from most adverse effects. Less recommended during fair weather due to potential boat activity.
    
2. **Water Temperature**
    
    Typically ranges from **18¬∞C in winter** to **24¬∞C in summer**, consistent with regional averages.
    
3. **Visibility**
    
    Average visibility is maintained even during poor weather, though it can vary based on recent conditions and water movement.
    
4. **Current and Surge**
    
    Generally minimal due to the sheltered nature of the site. However, divers should remain vigilant for boat traffic and possible surge near entry and exit points.
    

---

## üê† **Marine Life & Ecosystem**

1. **Marine Species Commonly Seen**
    - Various species of **soft corals** and **brain corals**
    - **Seahorses**, **cuttlefish**, and **nudibranchs**
    - Schools of **small fish**, including **catfish**, **morwongs**, and **parrotfish**
    - Occasional sightings of larger fish resembling **Samson fish**
2. **Habitat Type**
    
    **Artificial reef** environment created by man-made groins and jetty structures, providing shelter and feeding grounds for diverse marine life.
    
3. **Seasonal Marine Life Events**
    
    Warmer months may see increased activity of species like cuttlefish and seahorses. Specific seasonal events are not documented.
    
4. **Conservation Info**
    
    While not within a designated marine protected area, divers should respect local regulations, avoid disturbing marine life, and be cautious of fishing lines and boat traffic.

## üó∫Ô∏è **Dive Map**

1. **Dive Site Layout**
    
    The site comprises several man-made rock groins and a concrete jetty forming part of a sea defense system. Divers can explore along the groin walls, with depths increasing further out. Entry and exit points vary, with some requiring careful navigation over rocks.
    
2. **Community Notes**
    
    Divers have noted the site's suitability during poor weather due to its sheltered conditions. However, caution is advised regarding entry and exit points, potential boat traffic, and fishing activities.
